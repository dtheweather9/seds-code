Opportunity Title: How do Massive Stars Enrich the Interstellar Medium?
Opportunity Type: Internship

Students applying for fellowship opportunities will be required to submit a proposal to the mentor. Prior to submitting a proposal, students will create a proposal summary with a description of their idea, and they will submit it to the mentor for their approval. The mentor has 10 business days to respond to each proposal summary. It is anticipated that mentors will only approve a couple proposal summaries, and they will work with those students to create their proposals and to make sure the topic is mutually beneficial to both the mentor and student.

Spectroscopic data is available from Hubble Space Telescope, from Herschel Space Observatory, from European Southern Observatory's Large Space Telescope, possibly from the Atacama Large Millimeter Array, that will be analyzed for measures of atoms and molecules. These measures will be used in models of how molecules and dust forms and survives in the harsh environment near Eta Carinae, a massive binary close to becoming one or two supernovae, possibly even a hypernova or gamma ray burster.
A final report would be the minimum. The potential is for a poster presentation at a local meeting or the American Astronomical Society semi-annual meetings. The ultimate goal is a refereed paper leading to new insight.
Ability to learn and use existing programs written in Interactive Data Language (IDL); ability to read a scientific textbook and journal papers. Working knowledge of atomic and molecular spectroscopy.
