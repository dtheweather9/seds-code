Opportunity Title: Characterization of Cassini Composite Infrared Spectrometer (CIRS) - YEAR LONG
Opportunity Type: Internship

Students applying for fellowship opportunities will be required to submit a proposal to the mentor. Prior to submitting a proposal, students will create a proposal summary with a description of their idea, and they will submit it to the mentor for their approval. The mentor has 10 business days to respond to each proposal summary. It is anticipated that mentors will only approve a couple proposal summaries, and they will work with those students to create their proposals and to make sure the topic is mutually beneficial to both the mentor and student.

This graduate-level student internship project (Spring semester 2014) will focus on characterization and data processing of the Cassini Composite Infrared Spectrometer (CIRS), currently in orbit at Saturn. The technical objects are as follows: 1) characterization of the Cassini detector fields of view, using in flight and ground test data. Both empirical and analytical models will be determined for use by the science team. 2) improvements to the removal of electrical interferences (spikes) from the interferogram data. SVD/PCA and other techniques will be tested for efficacy. 3) laboratory testing of CIRS flight spare components, including laser diodes and detectors, to characterize performance degradation since the instrument launch in 1997. These tasks may be expanded/reduced in scope, or similar tasks added as necessary depending on the progress of the research work.
The student will be expected to write a final report before departing, detailing the results of investigations. Any computer code generated will be passed to the CIRS team and documented. Time permitting, the research work can be written up as scientific or technical reports for publication.
The student should have a strong computer background, including programming in a general-purpose compiled language such C/C++ or FORTRAN; and preferably also some scripting languages such as perl or python. Experience with data analysis software such as Matlab or IDL would advantageous. The student should already hold a bachelors degree with a major in physical science or engineering, however a detailed knowledge of space-borne instruments or CIRS in particular is not required and will be acquired during the internship.
